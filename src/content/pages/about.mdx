# I'm a UX leader and inclusive design advocate

My career spans more than two decades, and I've never been more excited for this work. More than ever before, we have the ability to improve people's lives through the digital experiences we create. At the same time, we're at a critical impasse: AI and automation can either expand access and opportunity or leave people behind.

Technologies come and go, but the principles and practices behind effective design remain the same. That's why I work to keep everything I do grounded in empathy and inclusion.

As a UX researcher, I know the value of gathering evidence and testing with real users. As a manager, I believe it's just as important to listen as it is to lead. As a disabled person, I know what it feels like to be left out.

I've worked across federal, state, and city government, national and international nonprofits, education, and healthcare. Whether I'm mentoring teams, leading research, or building design systems, my goal is to leave things better than I found them.
